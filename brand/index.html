01  The Mark

One Logo. A Lot of Attitude.

The Sound & Fury wordmark is built from a heavy, condensed, slightly distressed type — loud, hand-worn, and unmistakably ours. The signature move is the ampersand: a lightning bolt strikes straight through the &, tying the two words together and carrying the energy of the name.

The Lightning Ampersand

The & / lightning bolt is the heart of the identity. It is never replaced with a plain ampersand, removed, or redrawn. When the wordmark is used, the bolt comes with it.

Two official compositions cover almost every need:

Primary Lockup — the wordmark with the tagline "Local apparel printing that doesn't suck." and the web address. Use this as the default whenever there's room.

Wordmark — SOUND&FURY on its own, for tight spaces, icons, apparel, and anywhere the tagline would get lost.

04  Clear Space & Sizing

Give It Room to Breathe.

The logo needs a buffer of empty space so it never feels crowded or competes with other elements. And below a certain size the distressed texture turns to mud — keep it legible.

Clear Space

Keep clear space on all sides equal to at least the height of the "S" in the wordmark. More is always fine. Nothing — type, photos, edges — enters this zone.

Minimum Size

Don't reproduce the wordmark smaller than 120 px wide on screen (about 1 in / 25 mm in print). For the full lockup with tagline, keep it at 230 px wide or larger so the small type stays readable.

07  Usage Rules

Do This. Not That.

The fastest way to keep the brand looking sharp: protect the proportions, the colors, and the clear space. When in doubt, use a file straight from the asset pack and leave it alone.

Do Keep it clean

  • ✓ Use the supplied logo files at their original proportions.
  • ✓ Give the mark its full clear space on every side.
  • ✓ Use black on light backgrounds, gray or white on dark.
  • ✓ Pick contrast that lets the whole wordmark read clearly.
  • ✓ Scale proportionally — hold Shift, always.

Don't Mess with it

  • ✕ Don't stretch, squash, or distort the logo.
  • ✕ Don't recolor it outside the brand palette.
  • ✕ Don't add drop shadows, glows, outlines, or bevels.
  • ✕ Don't rotate the logo or rearrange the lockup.
  • ✕ Don't swap the lightning ampersand for a plain "&".
