Digitizing Done Right

Your Logo, Translated to Thread.

Every embroidery job starts with digitizing: converting your artwork into a stitch file the machine can sew. Bad digitizing is why some embroidered logos look like a blurry sweater. We digitize clean, so small text stays readable and curves stay smooth.

Once your logo is digitized, we keep the stitch file on hand. Reorders, new hat styles, a jacket run next season: same sharp stitch-out every time.

  • ⚡ Clean digitizing so your logo stitches sharp
  • ⚡ Stitch file saved for every future reorder

Good Questions

Embroidery, Answered.

More on the full FAQ page, or read screen printing vs. DTF vs. embroidery for the full method breakdown.

What can you embroider?
Hats, beanies, polos, button-downs, quarter-zips, jackets, and bags. If it holds a stitch, we can probably brand it. Structured garments and headwear give the cleanest results.
What is digitizing and do I need it?
Digitizing converts your logo into a stitch file the embroidery machine can sew. We handle it for you, and once your logo is digitized we keep the file on hand, so reorders and new garments use the same clean stitch-out.
Is there a minimum order for embroidery?
No. Order as few as one piece. Per-piece pricing improves with quantity, so tell us your target count and budget and we'll quote it straight.
Should I choose embroidery or screen printing?
Embroidery is the premium, durable choice for hats, polos, and outerwear where a stitched logo reads as quality. Screen printing wins for tees, large artwork, and bigger runs. Many brands use both: embroidered hats and polos, printed tees.

Ready to Stitch Something Sharp?

Tell us the garment, the quantity, and send your logo. A real person quotes it fast and we'll flag anything that won't stitch clean before you spend a dime.
